## The Answer to AI Slop Isn't Better Detectors. It's Better Formats

The Atlantic's "Science Is Drowning in AI Slop" (Jan 2026) + Richard Sever's
Newton Institute thread (Apr 2026) frame a clean angle: the entire AI slop
discourse treats this as a detection problem (build better AI catchers). The
structural argument is that opaque document formats (PDF) make fraud easy and
verification hard. Semantic, machine-readable formats make provenance,
reproducibility, and accountability legible by design.

Sever separately flags the "author-facing vs post-hoc verification" tension:
should AI checking happen during writing or after publication?
