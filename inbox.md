- [AI in scientific publishing: Slower, worse, and more expensive](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aek5570)
  H. Holden Thorp (Editor-in-Chief of Science and the Science family of journals), Science editorial, July 9 2026 (Science vol. 393 p. 225, DOI 10.1126/science.aek5570). Thorp inverts the "faster, better, cheaper" NASA-era slogan popularized by Daniel Goldin to describe AI's actual effect on scientific publishing. Key quote: "although AI is helping Science catch errors that can be corrected or elements that are missing from a paper, its use and the evaluation of the output require more human effort, not less." This is the sharpest institutional counter-narrative yet to the AI-productization thread edition 015 covered (Claude Science, Google PAT, Turner's DIY skill) and edition 016 covered (Biomni, DeepMind Co-Scientist, OpenScience, PaperBanana). The pairing worth naming: Science just published Biomni in July 2026 as peer-reviewed institutional endorsement AND its EIC published an editorial contradicting the AI-productization narrative in the same journal in the same window. Also pairs with Thorp's January 2026 "Resisting AI slop" editorial (Science DOI 10.1126/science.aee8267) as a running position from the same voice. Contrasts with the Nature side: Callaway's survey (edition 016) was shared by Nature EIC Skipper without editorial pushback, while Science EIC is publicly resisting. Two flagship journals framing the same category differently is itself the story.
