- [Rethinking Publication: A Certification Framework for AI-Enabled Research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22026)
  Lu, Karanjai, Xu, Shi 2026 (University of Houston). Normative-policy framework completing the AI-era publishing trilogy alongside Kwon ("Publish and Perish," formal dynamics) and Patel & Chan ("You can just review things," ethnography of informal peer review). Core move: decouple knowledge certification (does the work hold?) from human-contribution attribution (who made it?), which publication has historically performed simultaneously and which AI pipelines have separated for the first time. Two-layer architecture — Layer 1 quality (pipeline-agnostic, unchanged), Layer 2 contribution-grading (pipeline-sensitive: A=pipeline-reachable, B=required human direction at identifiable stages, C=required the consensus-contradicting abductive move). Direct rejection of detection-based responses ("the wrong layer") and disclosure-only policies (cites He & Bu 2025: 0.1% disclosure compliance despite 70% mandate). Strongest single quote: "decoupling certification from attribution is the policy catching up with a structural change that has already occurred." Three papers in three weeks (Kwon, Patel & Chan, Lu et al.) converging on the same structural diagnosis from different methodologies — the AI-era publishing reform thesis is consolidating.

- [Doing Vibe Physics: How GPT-5.x derived new results in theoretical physics and quantum gravity](https://www.latent.space/p/lupsasca)
  Latent Space podcast, May 2026. Guest: Alex Lupsasca (OpenAI). 1:31:50 on using GPT-5.x to derive original results in theoretical physics and quantum gravity. A concrete case study of an LLM contributing to frontier research rather than summarizing it. Pairs with Lu et al. above as evidence that the certification-vs-contribution split is being forced by actual practice.
