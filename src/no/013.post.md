Retracted papers persist on Wikipedia for a median of 3.68 years before correction. Health sciences: five years. A fifth of citations are added AFTER retraction without any warning. Every AI system retrieving from Wikipedia inherits that lag.

This week's Hallway Track sits at three layers of the same infrastructure problem. Todd Carpenter (NISO) names the vocabulary gap: "citation," "attribution," and "provenance" label different functions we routinely conflate. A Learned Publishing survey of 802 journals shows STEM and humanities drawing completely different lines on AI in peer review. Two arXiv papers, including a 57-page Google DeepMind report, redesign the paper format for AI consumption.

And against the "AI will ruin research" narrative Nature surveyed in social sciences this month, NEJM AI publishes the first peer-reviewed case of OpenAI's o3 helping Boston Children's clinicians solve 18 previously-unsolved pediatric rare disease cases. Capability is real. The infrastructure to evaluate it isn't.
