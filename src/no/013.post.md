We keep using "citation," "attribution," and "provenance" interchangeably when talking about AI and research. Todd Carpenter's piece in Scholarly Kitchen this week explains why that matters: they name different functions, and conflating them makes it harder to build AI tools that handle scholarly knowledge correctly.

Meanwhile, a Northwestern study finds retracted papers linger on Wikipedia for a median of 3.68 years, and a Learned Publishing survey of 802 journals reveals how differently STEM and humanities fields are drawing lines on AI in peer review.
