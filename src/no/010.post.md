Scientific Reports retracted two papers this month for AI-generated nonsense terminology -- "Newtonian drinks" for fluids, "floor anxiety" for surface tension. The terms passed peer review.

That pattern sits next to two preprints this week pulling in opposite directions on AI research agents. AutoScientists (Harvard) argues the bottleneck is governance, not capability. Tang and Yang show that the agents we have generate ideas that cluster tightly around existing work rather than opening new territory.

Also: ESMFold2 goes fully open-source with lab-validated protein binder design, and AI search tools are failing because the metadata they depend on is too thin.
