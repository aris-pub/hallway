A Columbia-led audit of 2.5 million biomedical papers, published in The Lancet this week, found fabricated citations in 1 of every 277 papers in early 2026. The rate has increased 12-fold since 2023, with the steepest climb beginning in mid-2024 as AI writing tools became widespread.

Meanwhile, the research discovery pipeline is shifting beneath us. The Scholarly Kitchen documents "zero-click readership," where AI overviews collapse the entire discovery cycle into a single step, and no one clicks through to the paper. Downloads and citations may no longer measure how work is actually consumed.

On the other end: Alex Lupsasca describes using GPT-5.x to derive original results in quantum gravity, and Lu et al. propose decoupling quality certification from contribution attribution in publishing. The gap between how research is being produced and how institutions track it is widening.
