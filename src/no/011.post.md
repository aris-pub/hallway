A fake paper appeared under Eric Topol's name in a journal he'd never heard of. Spinellis found the same thing happened to him, and that 48 of 53 papers in the same predatory journal appeared AI-generated -- some listing authors who had already died.

This is one thread from this week's Hallway Track. The other two: the sum-product conjecture becoming the second major pure-math conjecture broken by AI-derived techniques this spring, and OMB proposing to eliminate journal subscriptions and APCs as allowable federal grant costs effective October, while open-access mandates remain in force.

The identity layer, quality layer, and funding layer of scientific publishing are all under stress simultaneously, and the fixes being attempted in each operate independently of the others.
